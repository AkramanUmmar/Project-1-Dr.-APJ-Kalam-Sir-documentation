Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam Sir

(1935-2015)

Missile Man of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was an eminent Indian scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam was known as the "Missile Man of India" for his pioneering work in the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.

Dr. Kalam graduated from the Madras Institute of Technology in aeronautical engineering and joined India's Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO). He played a pivotal role in India's space and missile development programs, including the development of satellite launch vehicles.

Dr. Kalam's most significant contribution came in leading the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit. Later, as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister, he played a key role in India's nuclear tests of 1998.

Apart from his scientific endeavors, Kalam was a respected author, writing several books including "Wings of Fire," an autobiography which details his early life and career. His inspirational speeches and vision for India's youth made him a beloved figure across the nation

Dr. Kalam was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 1997 for his contributions to the scientific research and modernization of defense technology in India. Even after his presidency, he remained an active advocate for science, education, and societal development until his passing on July 27, 2015. Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam continues to be remembered as a symbol of integrity, wisdom, and inspiration for generations in India and beyond.

Biography of Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Sir

  • Birth: Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • Education: Graduated in aeronautical engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  • Career: Joined Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and later Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO).
  • Achievements: Led the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III). Pioneered India's missile programs, including Agni and Prithvi. Played a key role in India's nuclear tests of 1998.
  • Presidency: Elected as the 11th President of India in 2002, serving until 2007.
  • Post-Presidency: Continued to focus on education and youth empowerment.
  • Authorship: Wrote several books, including his autobiography "Wings of Fire".
  • Legacy: Known as the "Missile Man of India" and a prominent advocate for science and technology.
  • Honors: Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 1997.
  • Passing: Passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
